Navatar

Blind students face significant challenges when attending college

Besides having accessible books, navigating campus environments can be quite a challenge. Though outdoor GPS

User as sensor

Navatar seamlessly integrates with how blind users navigate familiar spaces as this includes the identification of known tactile landmarks. By having blind users confirm landmarks along their path, Navatar achieves a high localization accuracy at a low cost.

a;

Ubiquitous

Navatar has potential for large scale deployment as doesn't require extensive and expensive installation of sensors or beacons like other approaches (RFID/Bluetooth).

Low Cost

Navatar can run on any smartphone and Navatar can extract all information for navigation from indoor GIS maps, which most campuses environments seem to have available. Navatar is further open source, which keeps installation costs for campuses low and costs to students minimal as smartphone adoption is high among the blind population.
